Vegan Winter Stew (with Dumplings!)

Below is one of my absolutely favorite recipes to make in the colder months that are coming upon us. As the leaves start to change and every being starts to forage and hibernate, preparing this hearty soup certainly feel right. Below you will see a list of both the ingredients you need for the stew and for the dumplings.

For the Stew:

  • 3 TB Olive Oil
  • 1/4 Cup Flour
  • 1 Medium Onion, Sliced
  • 1 TSP Salt
  • 3 Cloves of Garlic, Minced
  • 6 Cups of Vegetable Broth
  • 2 Ribs of Celery, Sliced
  • 1 1/4 LBS Potatoes, Chopped
  • 1 Cup Carrots, Chopped
  • 2 TB Dill, Chopped
  • 1 TB, Thyme, Chopped
  • 1/2 TSP Paprika
  • A Few Pinches of Black Pepper
  • 1 1/2 Cups of Navy Beans

For the Dumplings:

  • 1 1/2 Cups of Flour
  • 2 TSP Baking Powder
  • 1/2 TSP Salt
  • 1 TB Rosemary, Chopped
  • 3/4 Soy or Almong Milk
  • 2 TB Olive Oil

Prepare the Stew!

First we will make a lower-fat roux. Preheat a large, heavy-bottom pot over medium-high heat. Add the oil and sprinkle in the flour. Use a slanted wooden spatual to stir pretty consistently for 3 to 4 minutes, until the flour is clumpy and toasty. Add the onion and the salt, adn toss to coat the onions completely in the flour mixture. Cook for five minute, stirring often. Add the garlic and stir for about 30 more seconds. Stream in the broth, whisking constantly to prevent clumping. Add the celery, potatoes, carrots, dill, thyme, paprika, and black pepper, then turn teh heat up and cover to bring to a boil. Keep close eye on it and stir often so that it doesn't clump or boil over. Once boiling, lower the heat to simmer and ley cook uncovered for 20 to 25 minutes, stirring occasioanlly, until the stew is nicely thickened and the potatoes and carrots are tender.

Prepare the Dumplings!

Sift the flour, baking powder, and salt together in a large bowl. Mix in the rosemary. Make a well in the center and add the milk and olive oil. Use a wooden spoon to mix together until a wet dough forms. When the stew is ready, mix in teh beans and plop spoonfuls of dough right on top of the stew. You should get about 14 dumplings. Cover the pot tightly and cook for about 14 more minutes. The dumplings should be nice and firm. Use your ladle to dunk them into the stew to coat them. Ladle the ste winto bowls, and top with the dumplings. Garnish with additional herbs and serve.
